Come visit Columbia, Maryland!

Columbia is a planned community unveiled by James Rouse on June 21st, 1967. One of the defining characteristics of Columbia is its composition of 10 small villages. Each village was designed to be fully self-sufficient, each featuring schools, shopping centers, recreational facilities, and a community center. These villages are frequently interlaced with extensive foot and bike paths that serve to further connect the villages to themselves and others. And if you can't find what you're looking for here, Baltimore, Annapolis, and Washington D.C. are all about a half an hour away!

Main Attractions

Merriweather Post Pavilion

This outdoor concert venue, nestled within 40 acres of woodland, has been among the top rated music venues in the United States several times.

The Mall In Columbia

Boasting over 200 shops and services, The Mall In Columbia (it's what they call it, don't ask me) is sure to have something for everyone, including...

Main Event

The Mall's all in one entertainment supercenter has an arcade, a bowling alleys, billiards tables, food and drink, and even laser tag!

Sky Zone

With over 15 activities to choose from, this trampoline park is an endless source of fun for the whole family.

Robinson Nature Center

For those seeking the serenity of nature over the hustle and bustle of downtown, look no further than Robinson Nature Center. (They have a planetarium!)

Centennial Park

Technically in Ellicott City, the 337 acre park is only about a 10 minute drive from Columbia, and can play host to any outdoor activity you can dream of.
